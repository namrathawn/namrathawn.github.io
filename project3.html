Carbon dots

Duration: 2021 - 2024 | Field: Materials Science, Carbon dots

Project Overview

This project focuses on the green synthesis and multifunctional application of carbon dots (CDs), a class of zero dimensional carbon based nanomaterials renowned for their tunable optical properties and biocompatibility. Green luminescent carbon dots (J-10) were synthesized from the medicinal plant Justicia wynaadensis using a cost effective one step hydrothermal method. A nanocomposite (JT-10) was further developed by incorporating TiO₂ nanoparticles derived from Lavandula leaves through a green synthetic route.

Innovation: Transformed a culturally cherished plant, Justicia wynaadensis, into green luminescent carbon dots synthesized and studied for the first time, enabling eco-friendly sensing of toxic heavy metals and biocompatible alternatives to conventional dyes.

Key Findings

Green Synthesis & Characterization

Established a green, hydrothermal synthesis strategy for carbon dots and TiO₂ nanocomposites using plant-derived precursors. The synthesized J-10 CDs and JT-10 TiO₂ nanocomposites exhibited intense green emission and excellent photostability, with excitation-dependent fluorescence and ultrasmall particle size (≈1.85 nm).

Optical Properties

Distinct photoluminescence lifetimes were determined: 5.80 ns for J-10 and 2.84 ns for JT-10. The materials demonstrated exceptional optical characteristics including tunable emission wavelengths and high quantum yields, making them suitable for diverse optoelectronic applications.

Biocompatibility & Biosensing

Verified high biocompatibility and strong DNA-binding capability, underscoring their suitability for biosensing and therapeutic platforms. The J-10 carbon dots demonstrated highly sensitive and selective Cd²⁺ detection, achieving a linear response from 1 nM to 1 M with a remarkably low detection limit of 5.235 nM, positioning them as promising candidates for environmental monitoring and heavy metal sensing applications.
