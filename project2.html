Non-linear optical materials

Duration: 2018 - 2020 | Field: Third order non linearity, chalcone

Project Overview

This project investigates a novel furan-based chalcone crystal, 1-(furan-2-yl)-3-(3,4,5-trimethoxyphenyl) prop-2-en-1-one (FT3MP), as an organic nonlinear optical material for next-generation photonic devices such as optical limiters, switches, and modulators. The work combines controlled slow-evaporation growth of high-quality single crystals with Fourier transform infrared spectroscopy to confirm functional groups and establish the molecular framework responsible for nonlinear response. Third-order nonlinear optical behavior is quantified using single-beam Z-scan measurements, linking molecular structure to macroscopic parameters such as nonlinear absorption, refractive index, and susceptibility, and evaluating whether FT3MP meets key criteria—large third-order susceptibility, favorable nonlinearity, and good optical transparency—for integration into practical photonic components.

Key Findings

  • Successfully synthesized and grew FT3MP single crystals via Claisen-Schmidt condensation process, yielding optically transparent crystals with well-defined morphology suitable for NLO measurements.
  • FTIR analysis confirmed the presence of characteristic chalcone functional groups and conjugated π–electron systems, supporting the design strategy for enhanced third-order nonlinearity.
  • Z-scan experiments (open and closed aperture) provided the nonlinear absorption coefficient β, nonlinear refractive index n₂, and third-order susceptibility χ⁽³⁾, revealing sizable third-order NLO response in FT3MP.
  • The magnitude and sign of the extracted NLO parameters indicate that FT3MP is a strong candidate for third-order photonic applications, particularly in devices requiring optical limiting or intensity-dependent refractive modulation.
